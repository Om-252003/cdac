instance types in ec2 :
 - T (e.g., T2, T3 ... i.e. t3 micro, t2 micro , etc): These are General Purpose, burstable instances. They are ideal for workloads with low-to-moderate CPU usage that experience occasional spikes in activity (e.g., low-traffic websites or development environments).
 
 - M (e.g., M5): These are General Purpose instances that provide a consistent, balanced baseline of resources. They are a good "default choice" for typical backend or enterprise applications where you need an equal proportion of CPU and memory.
 
 - HPC (Hpc family): This is a specific instance category designed for High-Performance Computing workloads that require very high CPU performance and network throughput for large-scale, complex computational tasks like scientific modeling and simulations.

		Note: Aurora technically has two flavors (Aurora MySQL & Aurora PostgreSQL), but AWS counts them under Aurora.
